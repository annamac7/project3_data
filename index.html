Anaylizing the STEM Gender Gap

Image by Unsplash.com

By Annie Rudisill

October 26, 2020

It is general knowledge that women face discrimination in STEM fields, but how much? This article examines the difference in the number of women in STEM compared to other fields as well as compares the difference in earnings between male and females in STEM fields.

According to data from the 2018 census that examined data from 2013-2018, women in STEM make up the second smallest percentage of working women in the US. With jobs in natural recources, construction, and maintenance are the smallest. The largest percentage of women are employed in sales and office jobs.

Inside the field of STEM, the number of women hardly scratch the number of men, especially in Computer and Math and Architecture and Engineering.

Role Male Female
Computer and Math 3,426,408 1,207,452
Architecture and Engineering 2,385,386 436,725
Life, and Physical, and Social Sciences 758,714 657,518

The average percent of women in STEM jobs in each state is around 26%. The lowest percentage being 19.2% in Utah and the highest in DC where women make up 40% of workers in STEM.
